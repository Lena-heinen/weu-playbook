Decision support
Priorities Protocol
Co-designed with field representatives across WEU. Shared orientation principles to reduce decision fatigue.
WEU Playbook›Priorities Protocol
What this is for: When your day fills up and everything feels urgent, these principles help you decide what actually matters first. They were shaped by the people doing the work — and they can be revised by them too.
1
Patient and customer first — always
If a supply issue, tool failure, or process delay puts a patient or customer at risk — skip the queue. Call your manager directly. This is never an overreaction. Why: patient safety is the non-negotiable condition of this role. No process overrides it.
2
Check the Wiki before escalating
If a colleague in another market has already navigated the same situation, the answer is probably already there. Search first — escalate if you don't find it. Why: the knowledge exists. The problem is it isn't always findable.
3
If a request has nothing to do with a patient or customer, it is not the priority
Administrative tasks and internal processes matter — but they do not take precedence over active customer situations. Triage by what is clinically and commercially consequential. Why: the role is a sales role. Everything else is support infrastructure for it.
4
Protect your selling time — especially in peak periods
Q1 push, Q2 review, Q3 ramp, Q4 AOP — each quarter carries its own pressure. Use the Initiative Calendar to know when your market is at peak load. You can say no to non-urgent internal requests during those windows. Why: initiative fatigue is real. Timing is part of the design.
